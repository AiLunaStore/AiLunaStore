# Delegation Pattern

The Delegation Pattern is a design and operational principle where a primary agent or person assigns responsibility for specific tasks to specialized sub-agents or team members rather than attempting direct implementation. This pattern enables scaling workflows, leveraging specialized expertise, and maintaining clear accountability chains. In agentic systems like OpenClaw, effective delegation is the core mechanism for orchestrating complex, multi-step tasks. It connects to [[Memory-System]] for tracking who owns what, and [[Knowledge-Management]] for knowing what to delegate and when.
