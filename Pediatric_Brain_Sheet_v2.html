Respiratory Distress
CROUP (Laryngotracheobronchitis)
Dx: Barky cough, stridor, URI prodrome
Mild: Intermittent stridor at rest
Mod: Stridor + retractions, agitated
Severe: Tripod, silent child, cyanosis
Rx: Dexamethasone 0.6 mg/kg PO/IM
Nebulized EPI (1:1000) if severe
Keep calm → cold mist, HOB elevated
BRONCHIOLITIS (RSV)
Age: <2 years (peak 2-6mo)
S/S: Wheeze, cough, tachypnea, retractions
Mild: SpO₂ ≥92%, no/mild retractions
Mod: SpO₂ 88-92%, moderate WOB
Severe: SpO₂ <88%, severe WOB, apnea
Rx: Suctioning, O₂, hydration
NO: Albuterol routine, steroids, antibiotics
ASTHMA SEVERITY
Mild: SpO₂ ≥92%, talks in sentences
Mod: SpO₂ 88-92%, talks in phrases
Severe: SpO₂ <88%, can't complete sentences
Rx: MDI Albuterol q20min × 3, then q1-4h PRN
Ipratropium q20min × 3 (first hour)
Oral Steroids: Prednisolone 2 mg/kg
EF: Magnesium sulfate if refractory
Seizure Management
STATUS EPILEPTICUS
≥5 min continuous seizure OR ≥2 in 24hr
0-5 min: ABCs, O₂, Glucose check
5-10 min: Lorazepam 0.1 mg/kg IV
OR Midazolam 0.2 mg/kg IN
10-20 min: Fosphenytoin 20 mg/kg
>20 min: Phenobarbital 20 mg/kg
>40 min: Anesthesia / ICU
SEIZURE SAFETY
☐ Time the seizure
☐ Position on side
☐ Clear area of hazards
☐ Do NOT restrain
☐ Do NOT put anything in mouth
☐ Check glucose
☐ O₂ via NC
FEBRILE SZN: Tx fever >38°C
BEFORE DISCHARGE: Neuro exam normal, eating/drinking, parents taught seizure safety
Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)
DIAGNOSIS
Glucose >200 + pH <7.3 + HCO₃ <18
Anion gap elevated, + ketones
Serum K may be NORMAL or HIGH
FLUIDS
0.9% NS 10-20 mL/kg bolus
Then D5 0.45% NS + 20 mEq/L KCl
Insulin drip 0.05-0.1 u/kg/hr
Goal: Dec BG 50-75 mg/dL/hr
Correct over 48-72hr SLOWLY
CEREBRAL EDEMA WARNING: HA, AMS, bradycardia, ↓LOC → Mannitol 0.5-1 g/kg, hold fluids, head up
