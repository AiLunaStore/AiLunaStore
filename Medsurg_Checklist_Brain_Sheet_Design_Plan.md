# MedSurg Checklist Brain Sheet Design Plan

## Overview
**Objective**: Design a checklist-based MedSurg brain sheet that replaces repetitive handwriting with quick-check options, covering 80% of common cases while leaving space for unusual situations. Includes a human outline diagram for marking medical equipment locations.

**Research Basis**: Common options nurses repeatedly write (mental status, IV fluids, mobility, pain scale, common meds, assessments).

**Deliverable**: Portrait 8.5×11" printable sheet with logical checklist categories, circle/checkbox options, and integrated human outline diagram.

## Core Design Principles

1. **Checklist Mentality**: Replace free-text writing with pre‑defined options (checkboxes, circles) for speed and consistency.
2. **Logical Categorization**: Group related parameters into intuitive sections (Mental Status, IV Fluids, Mobility, Pain Scale, Common Meds, Assessments).
3. **80/20 Balance**: Provide enough options to cover 80% of typical MedSurg cases, plus blank lines for unusual or patient‑specific details.
4. **Human Outline Integration**: Include a simple human silhouette for visual marking of IV sites, Foley catheter, wounds, drains, etc.
5. **ADHD‑Friendly Visual Design**: Clear hierarchy, color coding, minimal clutter, high contrast.

## Layout Specifications

### Page Dimensions
- **Orientation**: Portrait
- **Size**: 8.5" × 11" (standard letter)
- **Margins**: 0.25" on all sides (print‑safe)
- **Layout**: Two‑column format (left: checklists, right: human outline + notes)

### Section Structure (Left Column, Top to Bottom)

1. **Header** (Patient Identification & Shift Info)
   - Patient name, MRN, room, allergies, code status, isolation precautions
   - Nurse name, shift date/time, fall risk score

2. **Mental Status Section** (Green theme)
   - Orientation checkboxes: □ A&O x1 □ x2 □ x3 □ x4 □ Confused □ Lethargic
   - Additional: □ Alert □ Restless □ Agitated □ Sedated
   - Space for free‑text notes: ____________________

3. **IV Fluids Section** (Blue theme)
   - Common fluids: □ LR □ NS □ D5W □ D5½NS □ D5LR □ Other: ______
   - Rate: □ KVO □ 50 mL/hr □ 100 mL/hr □ 125 mL/hr □ 150 mL/hr □ Other: ______
   - Site: □ Left AC □ Right AC □ Left forearm □ Right forearm □ Central line □ PICC
   - IV status: □ Patent □ Infiltrated □ Discontinued □ New IV needed

4. **Mobility Section** (Yellow theme)
   - Ambulation: □ Ambulate □ Bedrest □ Up ad lib
   - Assistance: □ Assist x1 □ Assist x2 □ Wheelchair □ Walker □ Cane
   - Turning schedule: □ Q2h turns □ Q4h turns □ Pressure‑relief mattress
   - Fall precautions: □ Bed alarm □ Low bed □ Non‑skid socks

5. **Pain Scale Section** (Red theme)
   - Visual 0–10 scale with descriptors:
     0 (No pain) – 2 (Mild) – 4 (Discomforting) – 6 (Distressing) – 8 (Intense) – 10 (Unbearable)
   - Circle current pain level (0‑10)
   - Location: ____________________
   - Interventions: □ Medicated □ Non‑pharm (ice, elevation, etc.) □ Reassess in 30 min

6. **Common Medications Checklist** (Purple theme)
   - Frequent MedSurg meds with timing checkboxes:
     - □ Acetaminophen (q4‑6h) □ Ibuprofen (q6‑8h) □ Oxycodone (q4‑6h PRN)
     - □ Heparin SQ (q8h) □ Enoxaparin (daily) □ Insulin (sliding scale)
     - □ Antibiotic: ________ (q__h) □ Antiemetic: ________ (q__h PRN)
   - Space for additional meds: ____________________

7. **Assessments & Interventions** (Teal theme)
   - Neuro: □ Neuro checks q__h □ Pupils equal □ GCS ___
   - Wound care: □ Dressing change □ Wound vac □ Drain care
   - Drains: □ Foley catheter □ JP drain □ Hemovac □ Chest tube
   - Respiratory: □ O2 per NC □ O2 per mask □ Incentive spirometer q2h
   - GI/GU: □ NPO □ Clear liquids □ Regular diet □ Bowel movement

8. **Vital Signs & I&O** (Gray theme)
   - Compact grid for 4‑timepoint tracking (e.g., 0800, 1200, 1600, 2000):
     - Parameters: T, BP, HR, RR, SpO₂, Pain, Intake, Output
   - Cumulative I&O: Total Intake ________ mL, Total Output ________ mL, Balance ________ mL

### Right Column

9. **Human Outline Diagram**
   - Simple front‑view human silhouette (head, torso, arms, legs)
   - Landmarks: neck, shoulders, elbows, wrists, hips, knees, ankles
   - Symbols key:
     - ● IV site (with L/R notation)
     - ▲ Foley catheter
     - ✕ Wound/dressing
     - ○ Drain (JP, Hemovac, chest tube)
     - ■ Pressure injury
   - Space around silhouette for marking locations

10. **Notes & Plan Section**
    - Lines for narrative notes, shift plan, pending tasks, handoff highlights.

## Typography

- **Font family**: Segoe UI, system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif
- **Base font size**: 8pt (print optimized)
- **Section headings**: 9pt bold, uppercase
- **Checkbox labels**: 8pt regular
- **Human outline labels**: 7pt regular
- **Line height**: 1.2

## Visual Cues

1. **Checkboxes & Circles**: Square boxes [ ] for multi‑select options, circles ○ for single‑select (e.g., pain scale).
2. **Shaded backgrounds**: Light color behind each section for quick visual separation.
3. **Bold borders**: Thin border around each section.
4. **Icons**: Small symbols in human outline key.
5. **Zebra striping**: Alternate rows within vitals grid.

## ADHD‑Friendly Features

- **Color coding**: Immediate section recognition.
- **Checklist completion**: Satisfaction of checking boxes.
- **Minimal free text**: Pre‑filled options reduce writing fatigue.
- **Consistent layout**: Same order every shift.
- **High contrast**: Black text on light backgrounds.

## Human Outline Design Details

- **Silhouette style**: Simple line drawing, no anatomical detail.
- **Landmark dots**: Small dots at key locations (shoulder, elbow, wrist, hip, knee, ankle).
- **Symbol placement**: Nurse can draw symbol directly on silhouette or adjacent.
- **Left/Right notation**: L and R labels near shoulders/hips.
- **Space for notes**: Blank lines below outline for additional descriptions.

## Balance of Pre‑defined vs Free‑text

- **80% coverage**: Checklists derived from most common MedSurg nursing actions.
- **Blank lines**: Each section includes "Other:" or free‑text lines for exceptions.
- **Modularity**: Sections can be used independently—nurse can ignore irrelevant parts.

## Integration with Existing MedSurg Designs

- **Consistent palette**: Uses same color scheme as previous MedSurg/ER sheets.
- **Professional appearance**: Clean, no clip‑art, suitable for hospital environment.
- **Print‑ready**: No bleed, standard letter size, works with hospital printers.
