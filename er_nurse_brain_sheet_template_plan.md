# ER Nurse Brain Sheet Template Plan

## Overview
Emergency Department (ED) nurse brain sheet optimized for trauma and high-acuity patients. Designed for rapid assessment, real-time tracking, and efficient handoff. Landscape orientation (11×8.5") with 2‑patient layout to maximize detail per patient while keeping printable.

## Core Design Principles
- **Triage‑first:** ESI level visible at top, color‑coded by acuity.
- **Trauma‑ready:** Dedicated trauma scales (GCS, REVISED Trauma Score).
- **High‑frequency tracking:** Vitals columns every 5‑15 minutes, meds with timestamps.
- **SBAR‑structured handoff:** Built‑in SBAR sections.
- **Team coordination:** Trauma role assignments, procedure checklists.
- **Urgent data upfront:** Allergies, code status, isolation at top.
- **Trend‑focused:** Minimal long‑term I&O; emphasis on acute changes.

## 3. Color‑Coding Scheme

**ESI‑Based Acuity Colors:**
- **ESI 1 (Red):** Background highlight for urgent data section
- **ESI 2 (Orange):** Border or header tint
- **ESI 3 (Yellow):** Mild highlight
- **ESI 4 (Green):** Neutral
- **ESI 5 (Blue):** Neutral

**Section Colors:**
- **Urgent Data:** Light red tint (ESI‑dependent)
- **Trauma Scales:** Light gray background
- **Vitals Grid:** White with alternating row shades for readability
- **Medications:** Light blue tint
- **SBAR:** Light green tint
- **Procedures:** Light purple tint

**Print Considerations:** Use light tints (≤10% saturation) to conserve ink; ensure readability in black‑and‑white photocopies.

---

## 4. Visual Design Plan

### Typography
- **Header:** 10‑pt bold sans‑serif (Arial, Helvetica)
- **Section Labels:** 9‑pt bold
- **Body/Grid Text:** 8‑pt regular
- **Handwriting Space:** 8‑pt with generous line spacing

### Spacing & Alignment
- **Grid Lines:** Light 0.5‑pt borders between major sections, 0.25‑pt for inner grids.
- **Padding:** 2‑3 mm inside each cell.
- **Column Widths:** Proportional to content; vitals columns narrower, note columns wider.

### Printable Features
- **Bleed:** None (standard letter size)
- **Margins:** 0.5″ on all sides to avoid printer cut‑off.
- **Double‑Sided Option:** Back page could include additional notes, code cart checklist, or second shift continuation.

---

## 5. Implementation Notes

### Digital vs. Paper
- **Paper First:** Designed for handwritten use during high‑stress situations.
- **Digital Adaptation:** Could be implemented as a PDF form with fillable fields or integrated into EMR systems.

### Customization Options
- **Unit‑Specific:** Space to add unit‑specific common meds or procedures.
- **Pediatric Variant:** Replace trauma scores with pediatric scales (e.g., PTS).
- **Fast‑Track Variant:** Simplified version for lower‑acuity ESI 4‑5 patients.

### Testing & Validation
- **Pilot:** Test with ED nurses for usability, completeness, and time‑to‑document.
- **Iterate:** Gather feedback on section ordering, field necessity, and color utility.
