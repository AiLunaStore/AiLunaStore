# ER Nurse Brain Sheet Template Design

## Executive Summary
A printable, landscape‑oriented brain sheet designed specifically for Emergency Department nurses managing trauma and high‑acuity patients. The template incorporates triage prioritization (ESI levels), trauma scoring systems, high‑frequency vital sign tracking, time‑stamped medication administration, SBAR handoff structure, trauma team role assignments, and procedure checklists—all within a compact two‑patient layout that maximizes detail while remaining practical for fast‑paced ED environments.

---

## 1. Layout Specifications

### Page Dimensions
- **Orientation:** Landscape
- **Size:** US Letter (11″ × 8.5″)
- **Margins:** 0.5″ on all sides (safe zone for printing)
- **Effective Area:** 10″ × 7.5″

### Grid Structure
- **Two‑column layout,** each column dedicated to one patient.
- **Column Width:** 4.75″ each, with a 0.5″ gutter.
- **Vertical Sections** flow down each column, grouped by clinical priority.

### Section Order (per patient column)
1. **Urgent Data & Triage** (top – immediate visibility)
2. **Trauma Scales** (GCS, Revised Trauma Score)
3. **Vitals Grid** (high‑frequency, time‑based)
4. **Emergency Medications** (timestamped rows)
5. **Procedures / IV / Labs / Imaging** (checklist format)
6. **SBAR Handoff** (structured narrative)
7. **Trauma Team Roles** (when activated)
8. **Notes / Trends** (free text)

### Visual Hierarchy
- **Bold headers** for each section.
- **Light borders** between sections; heavier border between patients.
- **Color‑coded backgrounds** for acuity levels (ESI 1‑5).
- **Alternating row shading** in grids for readability.

## 3. Color‑Coding Scheme

### Acuity‑Based Highlighting
- **ESI 1 (Red):** Light red background tint for entire Urgent Data section.
- **ESI 2 (Orange):** Orange border around Urgent Data section.
- **ESI 3 (Yellow):** Yellow highlight of ESI box only.
- **ESI 4 (Green):** No highlight (neutral).
- **ESI 5 (Blue):** No highlight (neutral).

### Section‑Specific Tints (All Subtle, ≤10% Saturation)
- **Trauma Scales:** Light gray background.
- **Vitals Grid:** White with alternating light blue rows.
- **Medications:** Light cyan tint.
- **Procedures:** Light purple tint.
- **SBAR:** Light green tint.
- **Notes:** White.

### Print‑Friendly Considerations
- All colors are light enough to remain legible when photocopied in black‑and‑white.
- Borders are 0.5 pt solid lines; section dividers are 1 pt.

---

## 4. Typography and Spacing

### Fonts (Sans‑Serif for Clarity)
- **Header:** 10 pt Bold (Arial/Helvetica)
- **Section Titles:** 9 pt Bold
- **Body Text / Grid Labels:** 8 pt Regular
- **Handwriting Fields:** 8 pt with 1.5× line spacing

### Spacing
- **Cell Padding:** 2 mm inside each field.
- **Section Gutter:** 4 mm between major sections.
- **Line Height:** 1.2× for text, 1.5× for handwriting areas.

### Alignment
- **Labels:** Left‑aligned.
- **Checkboxes:** Inline with labels.
- **Grid Columns:** Center‑aligned for numeric data (BP, HR, etc.), left‑aligned for notes.

---

## 5. Printable Considerations

### Paper & Printing
- **Paper Size:** US Letter (8.5″ × 11″)
- **Orientation:** Landscape
- **Ink Usage:** Light tints, minimal solid fills to conserve toner.
- **Double‑Sided Option:** Back side can be used for additional notes or a second shift continuation (optional).

### Margins and Bleed
- **Safe Zone:** 0.5″ margin on all sides.
- **No Bleed** required (standard office printing).

### Durability
- Designed to be printed on standard 20‑lb bond paper; can be laminated for wet‑environment use.
- Font sizes are large enough to remain readable after photocopying or faxing.

## 7. Implementation Steps

### Phase 1: Design Validation
1. **Review with ED Nursing Staff:** Present mock‑up for feedback on field necessity, layout, and usability.
2. **Iterate:** Adjust section order, field sizes, and color choices based on feedback.
3. **Pilot Test:** Print sample templates for a limited clinical trial.

### Phase 2: Production
1. **High‑Fidelity Mock‑up:** Create in graphic design software (Figma, Adobe Illustrator).
2. **Printable PDF:** Generate fillable PDF form with locked labels and editable fields.
3. **Color‑Only and B&W Versions:** Provide both for different printing capabilities.

### Phase 3: Deployment
1. **Training:** Brief ED staff on template use, color coding, and SBAR integration.
2. **Print Stock:** Place pre‑printed templates in ED bays, triage areas, and trauma rooms.
3. **Digital Integration:** Explore integration with EMR systems for digital use.

### Phase 4: Sustainability
1. **Feedback Loop:** Establish a channel for continuous improvement.
2. **Version Control:** Maintain versioned templates with change logs.
3. **Unit Customization:** Allow minor adaptations for pediatric ED, fast‑track, etc.

## 9. Appendices

### Appendix A: ESI Level Definitions
- **Level 1:** Resuscitation – immediate life‑threatening
- **Level 2:** Emergent – high‑risk, confused, lethargic, severe pain
- **Level 3:** Urgent – stable, but requires multiple resources
- **Level 4:** Less Urgent – stable, requires one resource
- **Level 5:** Non‑urgent – stable, no resources required

### Appendix B: Revised Trauma Score Calculation
| GCS Score | Points | Systolic BP (mmHg) | Points | Respiratory Rate (breaths/min) | Points |
|-----------|--------|---------------------|--------|--------------------------------|--------|
| 13‑15     | 4      | >89                 | 4      | 10‑29                         | 4      |
| 9‑12      | 3      | 76‑89               | 3      | >29                           | 3      |
| 6‑8       | 2      | 50‑75               | 2      | 6‑9                           | 2      |
| 4‑5       | 1      | 1‑49                | 1      | 1‑5                           | 1      |
| 3         | 0      | 0                   | 0      | 0                             | 0      |

*RTS = GCS points + SBP points + RR points*

### Appendix C: SBAR Handoff Guidance
- **Situation:** What is happening right now? (e.g., “45‑year‑old male, MVC, GCS 10, BP 90/60”)
- **Background:** Relevant history, allergies, medications, baseline.
- **Assessment:** Current clinical impression, trends, concerns.
- **Recommendation:** What do you want the next provider to do? (e.g., “Continue fluids, repeat CT in 1 hour, call trauma surgeon.”)
